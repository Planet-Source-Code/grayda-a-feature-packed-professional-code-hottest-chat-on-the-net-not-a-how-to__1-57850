Welcome!

Thankyou for downloading NChat! If you have never used NChat before, then some explanation is needed! NChat is just like you favourite chat program, such as MSN Messenger, Yahoo! Messenger, ICQ and Yahoo! Chat. It has a big box up the top for all your incoming messages, and a small box at the bottom, for you to send messages from. It also has a box to the right, which shows you all the users in the room. NChat has many more features than your favourite chat program, such as:

Create your own chat room!
Insert and send over 60 picture smileys
Use WinMX Style (Third Person) actions
Have your own username, with your own picture next to your name
Earn points by chatting, and spend them in the store
Create your own colour-scheme, changing the entire look of NChat!
Plus heaps more!

NChat is perfect for use over your network at home, your network at work, or even your school computers! It allows unlimited users to join the chat, as long as your network can handle it! NChat cannot be used over the internet, but a new version is being developed to let people use NChat over the Internet!

With that box out of the way, you will see the main NChat screen, which will look something like this:


The big box is where all the messages will come in, while the smaller box down the bottom is where you send messages from. The tall box to the right is the list of users. For Beginners

Using NChat is fairly simple, if you have used MSN Messenger or something similar before. To send a message to EVERYONE in the room, click on the small box at the bottom, and type "Hello World!". When you are done, click the Send Button to the right, or press Enter. Watch in amazement as your message appears up the top, along with all the others. If no message(s) appear, then see Section 12 - Troubleshooting

To your right, is the user list. Everyone who is in the chat room you are in, is here. It looks like this:

Each person has their own username, and their own icon next to their name. You can change your icon by going to: Main Menu, Options (CTRL+O), then picking your icon from the drop-down box. If you find that someone else has an Icon that you can't select, then you need to purchase some more from the NChat Store (See section 5 - NCredits)

You can change your username in the same way. Just above the box to select your icon, is your username. You can change it for free, but there are limits to your username:

1) It MUST be less than 25 characters (Including spaces)
2) It MUST NOT have [A] in it (Because Administrators have this)
3) It MUST NOT have (A) in it (Because it looks a lot like [A])

Keeping this in mind, you can choose ANY name you want. Try not to make it offensive, or discriminating to others.

At the bottom right, is your Private Messages box. ALL Private messages you recieve, will appear here, as a small envelope, which looks like this:

You can view these messages by double clicking on them. If there are 2 or more from one person, all of the messages from that person will be opened.

1) Click Chat Rooms (Up the top), and then Create your own room


2) Fill in the vital details in the box:

3) Begin running your room!

It's that simple!

When your room is running, you will see a box much like this one

This box lets you run your room.

The Kick User button lets you eject users from the room. To use it, click the button, then click the username you want to kick. They will be kicked from the room

The welcome message is displayed whenever someone new enters the room. To set it, click, then enter the new message. If you want to use their username in the message (For example, Welcome New_Person!), then you enter the message as: Welcome, +newuser+! (With the plusses)

Profiles

A profile is like a second skin for NChat. It lets you change the colours, pictures and other stuff in NChat.

NChat comes with 14 Profiles built in, and can be found in the 'profiles' folder, in the same folder as NChat

If you don't like the profiles, then you can create your own using the in-built editor, available from the Main Menu under NChat Profiles and New Profile. Using the editor is simple. You just need to click the colour you wish to change, select a new colour, then click OK. You can preview your theme by clicking 'Preview'

All of the colours are as the text describes them. You can see what colours do what by clicking the Preview Button. When you are done, you can save them. The help button doesn't work ;D

If you have ever used WinMX, then you will be familiar with the file sharing system. You right click a username, click browse, then select a file to download. It downloads. NChat works like this.

All you need to do is right-click a file, and click download. When the file is downloaded, then it will tell you, and you can find the file in the NChat folder. You can tell how many files they have shared by looking next to their name: (All of them have got their sharing disabled)

You can pick a folder to share from the list box, and enable / disable sharing by clicking the check box. When files are shared, the number next to your name will change. The View Menu

The View Menu simply allows you to change your 'info' panel. There are 2 panels. The PM window, which has already been explained in the second section "For Beginners", and the Ignore Window.

The ignore window lets you ignore Private Messages from certain people. You can ignore people by right-clicking their name, and then clicking Ignore. You can remove the ignore by clicking View, then Ignore List, then right clicking the name you wish to remove, and click Delete Ignore. It's that simple!

Troubleshooting

Q: Why won't my NChat send / recieve messages?


A: This can occur because of several reasons, including a faulty / damaged network connection, you may be the only one in the room, You have switched NChat into Loopback mode (Only applicable to administrators). Try to use NChat on a network that works, with reasonable speed

 

Q: Why is it saying I am an NCredits Thief?

A: This message will be displayed if your NChat settings file has been tampered with. If you try, then NChat will pick it up, and tell you. This can also happen if NChat was interrupted while saving. Also, NChat settings files are only valid for one computer with one username (Eg. Grayda's file can't be copied over to Cheyenne's Computer because that is considered cheating)

 

Q: Why can't I select some user icons in the Options?

A: You need to buy those from the shop. THey are 50 NCredits Each. See the section on NCredits, for info on how to buy things from the NChat shop

 

Q: I have found a bug in NChat (i.e An error, then NChat closes), what do I do?

A: Contact Grayda at firestorm_visual@hotmail.com and describe the problem in as much detail as possible.

 

Q: I have a question that isn't in here, can you answer it for me?

A: ABSOLUTELY!! Just e-mail your question to firestorm_visual@hotmail.com

Glossary of terms

Administrator
This term is given to a person who has almost TOTAL control over the NChat Chatrooms. They Administer the room, and can (hopefully) keep the peace and keep the room in order

Loopback
Loopback mode in NChat allows you to run NChat on one computer. This is handy for me, because I can test the functions of NChat, with one computer. For those interested, NChat normally operates on 255.255.255.255, but in loopback, it runs at 127.0.0.1, limiting the number of computers it sends stuff to, to one. People running NChat with 2 or more people shouldn't worry, nor touch this setting

NCredits
Imagine if someone gave you 1 dollar for each time that you opened your mouth. You would be a millionaire! NCredits work just like that. Think of NCredits as Dollars, Pounds, Marks, Rupees or whatever currency you use. For each message that you send, you get 1 NCredit

Notch
Notch is short for NChat AutoBot Machine, and is NChat's own Bot. Think of a bot as an answering machine. If you are not home, then your message is played to everyone who rings. Notch can handle more than 1000 messages. When you type something (Which is called a trigger, and is like a phone number), the response is sent (Like the message on your answering machine). For example, if the Trigger is "Hello Notch", and the Response is: "Hello (Your Username)", then when anyone in the room types "Hello Notch", then "Hello (Username)" is sent as an everyday message, such as || Notch || Hello Bob!

Raw Data
Everything that is sent from NChat (ie. All messages etc.) are sent in Raw Data Form. Raw Data is a complex term for 'stuff sent from NChat'. All messages, actions, private messages etc., are sent as raw data

Welcome Message
This is a pre-set message, set by either the administrator or the creator of the room. When a new person connects to your room, that message is sent either to them, or to the whole room for them to read. A typical welcome message may look like this:
|| Welcome Message || Welcome to (The Room Name Here), (Your Username Here), enjoy your stay!!
